Current Obsession

A blog post written for the MIT 2019 Blogger Application.

What is your current obsession?



Minesweeper is the perfect blend of logic, math, speed, and fun. The simple design is easy to pick up but hard to master. In this essay, I will--

Alright, not gonna lie, Minesweeper is my all-time favorite game, and amongst all the other games on my phone, it’s the lone survivor. Wordscapes and Words with Friends barely lasted a month. 1010! is a recurring interest. But Minesweeper? I spent a good hour on it nearly every free day of winter break. By my standards, that’s an addiction.

And what makes it so good? It’s minimalistic, simple, easy to understand and learn. Bombs? Bad. Numbers? Number of bombs nearby. And it’s played purely by clicking. A good majority of addicting, successful minigames are built upon the easy-to-learn-hard-to-master principle. Minesweeper is a game designer’s role model.

If you’re not convinced yet, look to history. (Recent history.) Minesweeper was created to help new computer users learn how to click. We stan a game that’s not only fun but also helpful. ALSO it originates from the 1960s which means it has lasted all this time. She’s thriving.

Now, go ahead, give it a try. Join the dark side.
